Data for Impact: Keynote Recap

Ngao Labs Founder Oyori Obegi addresses university students on how analytics powers intelligent systems and shapes the future of work.

"Data is the raw material, but impact is the final product. We must never confuse the two."

How Analytics Powers Intelligent Systems

Recently, Ngao Labs Founder Oyori Obegi had the privilege of delivering a keynote address to university technology students. The topic, "From Data to Impact," focused on moving beyond syntax and code to understanding the real-world value of analytics in emerging economies.

Key Takeaways

1. Context is King
Data without context is just noise. The most successful data analysts are those who understand the business or social problem they are trying to solve, not just the math behind the model. We train our learners to be problem-solvers first, and coders second.

Oyori Obegi explaining the Ngao Labs mission to the next generation of tech talent.

2. The Future is Intelligent Systems
We are moving away from static dashboards toward systems that learn and adapt. The students of today must prepare to build systems that offer prescriptive insightsâ€”telling us what to do, not just what happened.

3. Ethical Responsibility
As builders of the future, we carry the weight of ensuring our systems are fair and unbiased. Tech is not neutral; it reflects the data it is fed, and it is our job to ensure that data is representative and ethical.

Ngao Labs remains committed to sharing these foundational insights with the broader academic community as we continue to democratize tech education across Kenya.
